For her PhD study, she is mostly interested in the following major directions:  1) develop new algorithms & designs for integrated circuits  2) apply knowledge of circuits to building sensors and systems that provide various functions.     

Past research has given me a full experience of mathematical analysis, circuit design, simulation and programming. And I would like to explore more about them as a PhD student.

Research

My past research topics include:

1.         Integrated circuit design:

1) a new self-powered P-SSHI circuit for  piezoelectric energy harvesting

I had a good experience of exploring both mathematical models and integrated circuit design

The research explores analytical models and new integrated interface circuit design for piezoelectric energy harvesting. I proposed a new self-powered P-SSHI rectifier based on MOSFETs and bipolar transistors. The circuit showed a higher power output in comparison with former ones (the standard full bridge circuit, those controlled by MCU and some recently proposed BJT-based or MOSFET-based ones). 

2) CMOS Integrated circuit for physical unclonable function (PUF)

I explored Monte Carlo simulations and other simulation analysis in this research

This research explores a Physical Unclonable Function (PUF) in integrated circuit with higher robustness against noises. It generates random bits which reflects variations in threshold voltage of MOSFET. Differential amplifier is utilized and noises (power supply noises and ground noises) are eliminated as common-mode input. The high performance is proved by noise analysis and Monte Carlo simulation
2.        Sensors & systems:

1) Circuit-experiment platform with a CompactRIO-based remote control

It is a full experience of mathematical analysis, circuit design, simulation and programming

This research explores an experimental system which will be extended to many other universities in China. The circuit system consists of a rebalanced loop of DTG, involving a DTG simulator which is a functional block with specific transform function, and a series of signal-processing circuits. And we are also building a compactRIO remote control system based on Labview. And The implementation involves exploring analytical models, signal-processing circuits as well as programming & simulation.

2) Eddy-current sensor for blade-tip clearance detection (National Innovation Project)

We worked in group to apply electromagnetism and simulation techniques to sensors

I was mainly responsible for the signal-processing circuit in this research. Also, I cooperated with other teammates in exploring the model of the sensor and the optimized shape of the head via simulation and controlled experiments. A host computer controlling interface based on LabView was also built.

3) Implementation of a simple electrocardiograph(ECG)

I made a helpful healthcare product using signal amplification & filtering circuits, though simple!

I conducted my own study to implement a helpful practical healthcare product, ECG. The system was built using DC regulated power supply, INA, Bessel filter and MCU-based programming. It turned out to be a low-cost practical device with good performance after tested in people.

3.        Algorithms

1) Vision-based Surface Measurement based on Focus Variation Technology    

I conducted my study to explore DTCWT for a new focus function and wrote a paper about it     



I proposed a new focus-measurement function based on the dual-tree complex wavelet transform considering its better direction selectivity. And the whole method involving image acquisition, focus measurement, Gaussian interpolation and a final checking was also explored by programming on MATLAB.
